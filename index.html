Terms Defined!

  • x : The initial stock price at the time of purchasing.
  • y : The final stock price. At the time of selling.
  • diff : denotes the difference between y and x
  • T : Denotes total amount (Rs.) invested in stocks

Derivation of above equation

Let's start with an amount 'x', the initial stock price at the time of purchasing stock.
Total amount you can invest = T
Total number of stocks bought = T / x

Now say y is the final stock price of the day.
Final amount gained/ loss = Total number of stocks * y = (T / x) * y

Net profit (assuming) = Final amount at end of day - Initial amount at start of day = ( (T / x) * y ) - T = ( ( y - x ) / x ) * T = ( (diff) / x ) * T
Here diff denotes the difference = y - x
Final profit = ( diff * T ) / x

Now (diff / x) denotes %change in market.

Deductions made

  • Profit depends upon how much you have invested, which is pretty obvious
  • Profit is only depends upon (%change) of the stock
  • Invest only in the reliable companies.
  • Start with low security. Less risk.
